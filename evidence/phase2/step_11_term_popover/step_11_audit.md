Corpus reference (snapshot's `第 398 ページ · 出現 2`):

  `surface.en = "Multi-Core Processor"`

  per glossary entry; the model echoed the surface as the kana_helper which
  was a minor deviation — see §3 below).
- 2 occurrences in the source corpus.

Coherence check vs corpus:
- ⚠️ The Japanese line's `（読み: マルチコアプロセッサ）` echoes the surface
  rather than the `reading` field `maruchi koa purosessa`. This is a model
  call — the `HOVER_SYSTEM_INSTRUCTION` example for プロセッサ uses
  「プロセッサ（reading: プロセッサ）」 form which is the same surface=reading
  pattern; the model is treating katakana surfaces as their own reading,
  which is technically harmless (a katakana word IS its own reading) but
  loses the romaji helper that screen-reader users would benefit from.
- ✅ 中文 + English lines mirror the corpus surface terminology.
- ✅ Definition is faithful and well-grounded — the "single CPU chip" + "two
  or more independent cores" framing is the canonical multi-core processor
  gloss.
- ✅ Total 98 output tokens — within ≤120-tok soft cap.

Verdict: **PASS with one cosmetic deviation** (the kana_helper.reading
romaji was not surfaced; the model treated the katakana surface as its own
reading). Not a semantic failure — just a small UX miss on the kana-helper
opportunity. Defer to Module D / Step 14 polish (potential SYSTEM_INSTRUCTION
tweak to always prefer the reading field when present).
